Assumptions the levers

Pay rises, inflation, a different envelope: open any phase in Plan and set its income or envelope directly — the forecast follows. Rates modelled: overdraft 29.0% EAR (daily), Amex 2.15% a month on the statement date, postgrad loan 7.3% a year. Flex living plans assume the 3-month 0% promotion, which needs each individual purchase to be £100 or more — smaller ones are only 0% if cleared on the next payment date. Left alone, a purchase defaults to 24 months at 24% APR. Amex minimums are computed from your statement formula: a month of interest, plus 2% of the balance, plus a twelfth of the annual fee (set to £650 — change it in the code comment if yours differs, or just edit the payment). The Barclaycard schedule assumes the old promo dies March 2027 and the £490 tranche rides at 0% to July 2027.

Backup move or recover your data

Your entries live in whichever browser address you opened this from, so they don't travel with the file. Before moving it to GitHub Pages — or a new phone — copy your data here and paste it in on the other side. Restoring replaces everything currently saved.
How it works: each phase has an everyday envelope — groceries, lunches, all flexible spending. Daily logs fill it; overspend and tomorrow's allowance absorbs it, underspend and it grows. Phase deficits roll forward instead of "failing". Phases roll over automatically on their dates. After 28 March the runway becomes Chapter 2 — the postgrad loan — then surplus months with room deliberately left to enjoy.
